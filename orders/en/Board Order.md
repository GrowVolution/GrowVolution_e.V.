<h1 align="center">Board Order</h1>

### § 1 Value of the Office
1. Each board member has the same rights and authorities.
2. In addition to their responsibility toward the general assembly, board members are also obligated to monitor each other's activities.

### § 2 Distribution of Tasks
1. The tasks of the board members are distributed according to the composition of the board. Delegation to association members and internal working groups is permitted through corresponding mandates.

### § 2a General Duties of the Board
1. The board manages the ongoing affairs of the association.
2. The board decides on the admission of new members.
3. The board represents the association in public and legal matters.
4. The board implements resolutions passed by the members.
5. The board organizes events, resources, and other means; it is responsible for smooth operations and appropriate handling.
6. The board maintains the association’s infrastructure and coordinates its further development.
7. The board supervises the association’s activities and is responsible for ensuring compliance with the principles of the statutes.
8. The board is responsible for moderating general assemblies and for keeping minutes.

### § 2b 1st and 2nd Chairperson
1. The chairpersons are responsible for issuing mandates.
2. When the board is minimally staffed, the chairpersons assume all general duties and distribute them fairly among themselves through mutual agreement.

### § 2c Treasurer
1. The treasurer handles the bookkeeping.
2. The treasurer coordinates payment transactions and is responsible for obtaining quotations and concluding corresponding contracts.

### § 2d Secretary
1. The secretary manages and handles the association’s correspondence.
2. The secretary keeps minutes during meetings.

### § 2e Permanent Representative
1. The permanent representative substitutes for absent board members and assumes their duties during their absence.

### § 3 Board Meetings
1. The board meets regularly to discuss internal board and association matters to remain informed of the current status and to optimize processes when necessary.
2. Agreements and information relevant to the members must be made transparent without delay.
